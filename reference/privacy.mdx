<Note>
  **Zero Data Policy** — KeyForge guarantees complete non-interference with your data. Every password you generate stays on your device. Nothing is ever transmitted to any server.
</Note>

This policy describes what data KeyForge does and does not collect, how the app handles your browser storage, and what security guarantees underpin the generation engine.

## Policy sections

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="1. No data collection">
    All service functions run exclusively on your device using the browser's built-in `crypto.getRandomValues()` API. KeyForge does not collect, process, or store any personal information whatsoever.

    There is no registration, no account, and no form that submits data anywhere. The passwords you generate exist only in your browser's memory until you copy them.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="2. No cookies">
    KeyForge operates with zero cookies. No tracking cookies, no analytics cookies, and no session cookies are set.

    The only browser storage used is `localStorage` for your theme preference (`dark` or `light`). This value never leaves your device and contains no personally identifiable information. You can clear it at any time through your browser's developer tools or site settings.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="3. No telemetry">
    No monitoring of user activity occurs. Zero information about your interactions — including which generator mode you use, what settings you configure, or what passwords you generate — is transmitted anywhere.

    Every password you generate stays in your browser only. There is no analytics script, no error tracking service, and no usage beacon of any kind embedded in KeyForge.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="4. Fully client-side architecture">
    KeyForge has no backend server. No requests are processed on remote servers at runtime. The app functions completely offline once the page has loaded — you can disconnect from the internet and continue generating passwords without interruption.

    Your generated passwords never leave your device. The generation engine (`pasgen.js`) and the entropy engine (`entropy.js`) both execute entirely within your browser's JavaScript runtime.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="5. Cryptographic security">
    All randomness is sourced from `crypto.getRandomValues()`, the browser's cryptographically secure pseudo-random number generator (CSPRNG). This is the same source of entropy used by TLS, SSH key generation, and other security-critical systems.

    `crypto.getRandomValues()` is seeded by the operating system's entropy pool (e.g., `/dev/urandom` on Linux). It is not a pseudo-random math function and cannot be predicted or reproduced from its output.

    The passphrase, PIN, and Phonetic Shield generators all draw from the same CSPRNG. The Fisher-Yates shuffle used to randomize character order also uses `crypto.getRandomValues()` at every step.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="6. Policy updates">
    We reserve the right to update this policy. All changes will be published on this page. The date of last update is shown at the bottom of this page.

    Because KeyForge has no user accounts and no contact information, we cannot notify you directly of changes. Check this page periodically if you rely on these guarantees.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
