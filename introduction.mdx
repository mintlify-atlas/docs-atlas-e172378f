## What is KeyForge?

KeyForge is a free, open-source web application that generates strong passwords, passphrases, PINs, and phonetic passwords directly in your browser. Every password is generated using `crypto.getRandomValues()` — the browser's cryptographically secure pseudo-random number generator (CSPRNG), the same source used by TLS and other security-critical systems.

KeyForge never sends your passwords anywhere. There is no backend server. No analytics. No cookies. Your generated credentials stay on your device.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open KeyForge">
    Visit [keyforgegd.vercel.app](https://keyforgegd.vercel.app) in any modern browser. No sign-up or installation required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a generator mode">
    Select **Password**, **Passphrase**, **PIN**, or **Phonetic Shield** from the tab bar at the top.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure your settings">
    Adjust length, character classes, anti-bruteforce guards, and other options for your chosen mode.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate and copy">
    Click the **Generate** button (or press **Ctrl+Enter**) to generate a password. Click the copy icon or click the output field to copy it to your clipboard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Privacy guarantee

<Note>
KeyForge operates with a **Zero Data Policy**. All generation runs in your browser using `crypto.getRandomValues()`. No passwords, settings, or usage data are ever transmitted to any server. The only browser storage used is `localStorage` for your theme preference.
</Note>

## Open source

KeyForge is fully open source. You can inspect every line of the generation logic on [GitHub](https://github.com/Gosiverua/keyforgegd).
